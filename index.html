Welcome!

My name is Sumedh.

I am a 2nd-year Computer Science student at the Georgia Institute of Technology. As I work on more personal projects and gather experience, this site will serve as my personal coding portfolio.

Since I decided to pursue a career in computer science as a 14-year old, I have worked on developing my programming and technical skills in and out of the classroom. I am most experienced in Java, having used it extensively as a programmer on FIRST Robotics Competition Team 1261 for 4 seasons (serving as lead programmer in my senior season), but I have recently worked on multiple projects in Python, and this seems to be the language that I will be using the most in the long run. I have also experimented with TensorFlow, Flask, and Android development. As seen with this webpage, I have also done some work with web development and Bootstrap. I recently completed an internship at Coca-Cola, and while I cannot disclose any specific details about the exact projects I worked on or display demos of what I have developed, I created a web app in Flask, HTML/JS/CSS, and JointJS (for visualizing flowcharts), and I experimented with Natural Language Processing, particularly the Word2Vec, Doc2Vec, and Bag of Words algorithms.

On campus, I am a member of HyTech Racing and the RoboCup subteam of RoboJackets, Georgia Tech's competitive robotics organization. In addition, I compete in Quiz Bowl as part of Georgia Tech's quiz bowl team, for which I am currently developing a new website. As a member of the 2018 Grand Challenges Cohort, I was on a team that was developing a solution for increasing the safety of electric scooters. Outside of academics and student organizations, I am an electronic music aficionado, and I have been DJing and producing electronic dance music for 3 years.

I attend numerous hackathons, and I am a participant in Google's Kick Start and Code Jam competitions. I recently attended PennApps XX in Philadelphia, PA, where I worked on a Chrome extension to detect sensationalized news articles. At the latest HackGT, I worked on the frontend API (in Flask) of a web app to help students find new friends in their courses/majors.

This fall, I am taking classes in Computer Organization, Objects & Design, and Robotics/Perception. However, I am most excited about being part of the current cohort of the Idea2Prototype branch of CreateX, Georgia Tech's on-campus student entrepreneurship initiative, in which I am developing a parking reminder app.

Thank you for visiting my page. Currently, you can download the most updated copy of my resume and access my Github profiles, and check out a few of my personal projects, and I hope to have some demos of those technical projects available soon.
